# PICKLE-LESSON-STANDARD — Best Practice Lesson Plan Framework

## Source: John Butler Primary College Instructional Model (WA)
Verified against: AERO "Teach Explicitly", NSW "What Works Best 2025", QCAA AC9 Unit Plan Template.

This is the gold standard for PickleNickAI lesson plans. All generated lesson plans MUST follow this structure.

## The 7-Phase Instructional Sequence

### PHASE 1: Daily Review (5-10 min)
- Spaced retrieval practice — material from days/weeks ago, not just last lesson
- Interleaved practice — random mix of prior content
- Paced, short, repetitive
- Use CFU (Check for Understanding): pop sticks, whiteboards, pair-share
- Student engagement norms apply
- Embed differentiation informed by formative assessment

### PHASE 2: Introduction / Hook (5-10 min)
**Must include ALL of:**
- **WALT** — "We are learning to..." (explicit learning intention)
- **TIB** — "This is because..." (purpose and relevance)
- **WILF** — "What I am looking for..." (success criteria)
- Hook — engage students, connect to prior knowledge
- Activate background knowledge (reduces intrinsic cognitive load)
- CFU: ask questions, check understanding

### PHASE 3: I Do — Focussed Instruction (10-15 min)
- Break learning into small steps / task analysis
- Modelling, demonstration, think-aloud, metacognitive strategies
- **WAGOLL** — "What A Good One Looks Like" — provide examples AND non-examples
- Use worked examples
- Resources and manipulatives (concrete → representational → abstract)
- Manage cognitive load — reduce extraneous load, increase germane load
- CFU often: questioning, whiteboards, ask non-volunteers, students explain thinking
- Use Tier 2 and 3 vocabulary in student responses

### PHASE 4: We Do — Guided Practice (10-15 min)
- Whole-class OR differentiated groups/pairs guided by teacher
- Robust questioning: literal, inferential, evaluative
- Prompting and scaffolding to guide and support
- **80%+ success rate / mastery learning** — if below, go back and re-teach before next step
- High-quality feedback: specific, immediate, constructive
- Differentiation: adjust instruction, curriculum, teaching strategies, resources
- CFU: questioning, observation, work samples, checklists

### PHASE 5: You Do (Together) — Collaborative Learning (10 min)
- Small groups or pairs applying new learning
- Reduced teacher guidance — teacher steps back
- Step back in when additional guided instruction needed
- Problem-solving and reasoning focus
- Continue differentiation
- Feedback to groups/individual students
- CFU: observe, listen, question, student explains thinking/reasoning

### PHASE 6: You Do (Independently) — Independent Learning (10-15 min)
- Independent application to consolidate and demonstrate understanding
- differentiation applies
- Provide feedback
- CFU

### PHASE 7: Plenary / Review & Reflect (5-10 min)
- Review and reflect to consolidate key learning
- Highlight relevance
- Determine what students have learned → inform future planning
- Use exit tickets, questioning, self-reflection, peer assessment
- Ensure non-volunteers are included

## Required Output Format

Every lesson plan MUST include:

### Header Block
```
WALT (Learning Intention): [specific, observable outcome]
TIB (Purpose): [why this matters — real-world or curriculum relevance]
WILF (Success Criteria): [what a student who has mastered this looks like]
AC9 Code: [relevant content descriptor code]
Year Level: | Subject: | Duration: | Date: | Lesson Type:
```

### Materials Section
- Specific equipment / manipulatives
- Handouts / worksheets (describe content, not just "worksheet")
- Digital resources
- Mentor texts (if relevant)
- Everything the teacher needs to prepare

### Lesson Sequence Table
| Phase | Duration | Teacher Does | Students Do | Resources | CFU Check |

### Differentiation
- EAL: visual word wall, sentence starters, home language labels, step-by-step with pictures
- Gifted: extension challenges, higher-order questioning, open-ended tasks
- Additional Needs: reduced demand, visual schedules, partner support, pre-scored materials

### Assessment
- Formative CFU checkpoints throughout
- Exit ticket at end (describe exact task + success criteria)
- Note how data informs next lesson

### Follow-Up Suggestions (at the bottom of every plan)
"Ask me to: [1] Generate a quiz [2] Create an exit ticket [3] Write a differentiation version for [EAL/gifted/additional needs] [4] Build a hot/cold task pair [5] Suggest word problems"

## Assessment Criteria (from Hot/Cold Task Spreadsheet)

For English/persuasive writing, use these 11 criteria:
1. Paragraphing
2. Punctuation
3. Spelling
4. Cohesion
5. Persuasive Devices
6. Vocabulary
7. Sentence Structure
8. Audience
9. Ideas
10. Text Structure
11. Total Score (out of 48 — 4 points per criterion, max 4 per criterion)

Cold task = pre-assessment (before unit)
Hot task = post-assessment (after unit)
Comparison shows growth.

For maths: automaticity, procedural fluency, problem-solving, reasoning, communication.
For science: understanding, inquiry skills, data interpretation, explanation.

## Mastery Threshold
**80% rule:** If students are not achieving 80%+ success during guided practice, do NOT move to independent work. Go back, re-teach, scaffold, then try again.

---

## Language to Use
- WALT / TIB / WILF — always
- CFU (Checking for Understanding) — throughout
- WAGOLL — when showing examples
- Learning Pit — for productive struggle
- Gradual Release — for ownership transfer
- Tier 2/3 vocabulary — in student responses
- Explicit instruction language: "I do → We do → You do together → You do independently"
